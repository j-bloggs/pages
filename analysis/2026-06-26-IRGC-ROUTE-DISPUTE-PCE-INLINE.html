New Complication: IRGC Route Authority Dispute
What changed tonight

Oman's IMO-coordinated corridor represents a fundamentally different approach from the IRGC's bilateral clearance model. Oman sought to route around Iranian territorial waters and IRGC enforcement — essentially creating a UN/IMO-backed transit pathway that Iran couldn't veto.

Iran's response: Full rejection. IRGC explicitly asserted that only routes approved and monitored by the IRGC Navy are valid. This is not a minor bureaucratic dispute — it's Iran claiming sovereign authority over the international waterway.

Implication for July 31 market: The resolution criteria (IMF Portwatch 7-day moving average of 60+ transits) depends on ships actually using the strait. If IRGC enforcement action deters vessels from using non-IRGC routes, and IRGC routes are perceived as unsafe (mines), traffic won't recover to 60+. The Oman corridor attempted to solve this by going wide of Iranian territory — IRGC killed it.

The market correctly stayed at 58.5% rather than holding the 69.5% peak. 58.5% reflects approximately the "mine clearance only" scenario with no route authority resolution. A breakthrough on route authority would justify 70–75%.
