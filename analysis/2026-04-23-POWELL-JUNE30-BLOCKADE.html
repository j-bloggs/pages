Before today's development: The June 30 market at 90.5% priced the succession as effectively resolved — Powell exits May 15 (natural term), Warsh confirmed sometime in May–June, clean handover. Markets had priced a "headless Fed" window of at most ~2–4 weeks (May 15 → mid-June).


After the DOJ appeal: The Warsh confirmation path is blocked for months — not weeks. The DOJ probe (now heading through D.C. Circuit appeal) means Tillis cannot relent before September at the earliest. This creates three possible scenarios:


Scenario A (now more likely): Powell as acting chair through Q3 2026. Warsh not confirmed by June 30; Trump designates Powell as acting chair (perhaps under legal or Senate pressure) to avoid a vacant-chair crisis. "Headless Fed" extends to September+ window. Probability: ~20% per market signals (May 14 at only 1.9% bounds the implied firing probability). Gold would sell off sharply on "certainty" signal; USD firms temporarily.
