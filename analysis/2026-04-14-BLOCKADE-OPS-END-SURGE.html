The Catalyst: US Naval Blockade of Iranian Ports

What happened

After Islamabad talks collapsed on April 12, Trump announced — via Truth Social and CENTCOM — that the US Navy would blockade all maritime traffic entering and exiting Iranian ports, effective April 13 at 10 AM ET. CENTCOM confirmed implementation. This is distinct from the bombing ceasefire (which remains technically in force) — there are no airstrikes resuming, but a full maritime blockade constitutes active military pressure.

  • Dual blockade: Iran's toll regime on westbound tankers + US Navy interdiction of all Iranian port traffic. Net effect: Iranian ports effectively closed from both directions.
  • Iran response: Tanker traffic to/from Iranian ports halted within hours of the announcement.
  • Trump on new talks: "I don't care" whether Iran returns to the table. The blockade is the pressure tool; Trump is not offering an off-ramp.
  • Ceasefire status: Bombing ceasefire still "holding well" per Trump — the blockade is additive pressure, not a ceasefire breach.

The mediator scramble (Axios, April 13) — now in context

Oman, Qatar, and Pakistan scrambled to revive nuclear talks because the blockade created a new, acute timeline. Iran's economy cannot sustain blocked ports through April 22. Mediators are bridging the five structural gaps (Hormuz sovereignty, enrichment, reparations, Lebanon scope, asset sequencing) before the ceasefire expiry hardens into a permanent blockade state. This is not independent good news — it is the market pricing a potential response to blockade pressure.
