This matters because China is not just privately lobbying Iran in this scenario. China is publicly committing its diplomatic prestige — in front of the world, one week before Trump arrives in Beijing — to demanding Hormuz open. If Iran refuses and Trump arrives to a still-blocked strait, China's role as indispensable mediator is undermined. Iran cannot easily say no without damaging its most critical bilateral relationship.

Iran's readout omitting the Hormuz demand is not reassuring — it is the tell. Iran is actively resisting China's preferred framing. But in this diplomatic triangle, silent resistance is weaker than public commitment. China wins the narrative war by being on record.

A pre-summit Hormuz announcement lets him arrive in Beijing claiming he solved the Middle East crisis, give China credit for mediating, and declare a summit win. That is a better deal than arriving with Hormuz still closed.
