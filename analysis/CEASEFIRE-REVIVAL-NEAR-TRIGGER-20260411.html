Key Cross-Market Tension: Ceasefire vs. Hormuz Divergence

⚡ Structural Divergence — Do Not Conflate These Two Markets

The ceasefire at 66.6% and Hormuz normalization at 19.5% are increasingly diverging. If Lebanon peace improves, Hezbollah's leverage over Iran to keep Hormuz closed weakens. Hormuz should start repricing upward — but it isn't.

This tells us: the market views the Hormuz blockade as driven by Iran's own interests (toll revenue, reconstruction leverage) independent of the Lebanon front. Iran's $1M+/vessel toll regime is a revenue stream, not just a military tactic.

Implication: Even if ceasefire revival fires (>70%), do NOT expect Hormuz to immediately follow. The Hormuz trade (19.5% → 25–30%) requires nuclear deal progress, not just a Lebanon ceasefire. The oil floor narrative persists even in the ceasefire bull scenario. WDS/STO remain structurally supported.
