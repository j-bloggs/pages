🔍 Analysis

What the Market Is Saying

The -14pp drop does not mean oil is crashing. WTI spot remains ~$106 with Hormuz closed — the structural premium is intact. What the market is re-pricing is the probability of a spike to $120+ during May. The War Powers deadline creates a new path: if courts enjoin the blockade operations, or Congress forces a vote and loses, the US naval presence could be legally challenged — potentially accelerating the Hormuz opening timeline. That reduces the "sudden escalation spike" scenario probability.

The "Trump ends ops June 30" market falling simultaneously (−5pp/hour) is the counter-intuitive signal: a War Powers legal challenge actually makes it harder for Trump to voluntarily announce ops-end, because doing so would be politically framed as capitulation to congressional pressure. Trump is more likely to contest the legal challenge through courts while maintaining the blockade, rather than voluntarily announcing withdrawal. Markets are pricing lower probability of a clean formal announcement.
