The 20:41 alert framed the Jask incident as the first confirmed kinetic exchange since April 7. Overnight, US CENTCOM flatly denied any ship was hit — Iran's claim is consistent with warning shots or state media exaggeration during an operationally tense Project Freedom transit. This is not unprecedented: Iran has made similar unverified claims during Hormuz blockade enforcement since April 12.

The oil market is the ground truth. WTI spot fell from ~$106.50 to ~$101.60 overnight — the market is reading the Jask incident as contained/disputed, not as a confirmed escalation. WTI $110 May pulling back from 92.5% to 81.5% is consistent with this: still highly likely spot touches $110 at some point in May (we are only $8.40 from the threshold), but the peak panic pricing overstated the risk.

Project Freedom continuing despite Iran's objections is actually a moderate positive for Hormuz de-escalation: if US escorts successfully transit the Strait, the blockade's practical hold loosens and commercial confidence may return, increasing Hormuz lift probability over time. The near-term June 30 market at 38% captures this optionality.

Key watch: The test of the Jask interpretation is Project Freedom's completion. If US-escorted ships successfully transit without further incident and return to a safe zone, WTI $110 May should continue drifting down from 81.5%. If Iran fires again (confirmed hit), expect immediate re-spike toward 90–95% and a WTI $120 move above 50%. Do not pre-position on either scenario — wait for confirmation.
