Bear Case Reversal: -7.0pp in 30 Minutes

The WTI $90 bear case — that oil falls to or below $90 by end-April — was our primary thesis since April 7 and peaked at 78.5% intraday. It has since reversed -7.0pp to 71.5% between the 07:30 and 08:00 AEST collections.

Critically, this reversal is NOT accompanied by large moves in $140/$150/$200. The $130 corridor is absorbing the probability mass migrating from the bear case. This signals the market is repricing to an uncertain $90–$130 range — consistent with WTI spot in the $95–$115 region with outcome contingent on whether Iran re-enters Lebanon.

Why the Trigger Fired: Lebanon Catalyst

The WTI $130 probability has near-doubled from ~8–9% one week ago to 18.5% today. The move accelerated on April 8–9 as:

  • Israel struck 100+ targets in Lebanon (April 8), explicitly stating the US-Iran ceasefire "does not cover Lebanon"
  • Pakistan PM ceasefire claim repudiated: PM announced the ceasefire covered "Lebanon and elsewhere"; Netanyahu's office disputed this within hours
  • Israel-Hezbollah ceasefire collapsed from 84.9% intraday peak to 30.1% — a -54.8pp crash in under 12 hours

The $130 scenario hinges on Iran re-entering the conflict over Hezbollah, forcing resumed Strait of Hormuz disruption.

Cross-Market Contradiction

US-Iran ceasefire durability at ~100% vs Israel-Lebanon ceasefire collapsing toward 25%
The market simultaneously prices the US-Iran ceasefire as ironclad, the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire as marginal, and WTI $130 at a non-trivial 18.5%. For all three to coexist, Iran must permanently abandon Hezbollah under fire. That's a possible but significantly overpriced scenario.

The asymmetric position: The US-Iran ceasefire (near 100%) is the most overvalued market in our universe. A 5% probability on ceasefire breakdown would imply +30–50pp on WTI $130. Iran has genuine prestige costs from abandoning Hezbollah, particularly given succession dynamics post-Khamenei.
