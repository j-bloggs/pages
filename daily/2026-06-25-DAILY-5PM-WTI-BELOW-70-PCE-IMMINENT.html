Key Stories

🛢️ Story 1 — WTI Below $70: Market Pricing Through IRGC Rhetoric

WTI crude dropped to $69.13/bbl today, down $3.22 (−4.5%) from yesterday's $72.35 — the lowest level since late February and the largest single-session decline in weeks. The stated catalyst: increasing tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz and continued progress in US-Iran peace framework implementation.

The critical cross-market read: on June 20, Iran's IRGC re-declared the strait closed and warned vessels not to approach, citing Israeli Lebanon strikes as a ceasefire violation. Yet WTI is falling. Markets are pricing through the IRGC rhetoric — the MOU framework (signed June 15-17 at G7/Versailles) provides legal cover for US-supervised transit, and CENTCOM confirmed 25 vessels transited on June 23, 55 on June 21. When oil falls alongside an IRGC closure declaration, the declaration is not credible. WTI below $70 is the ground truth: physical supply is normalising.

Note: WTI (proxy for Brent) for Australian fuel pricing. Brent typically trades $2–4/bbl above WTI, so Brent ~$71–73/bbl. At these levels, Australian petrol floor moves toward A$1.80–1.88/L — a decisive break from the A$2.00+ crisis peak and a further decline from this morning's A$1.88–1.95/L estimate.

📊 Story 2 — PCE Tonight 10:30PM AEST: Prediction Markets Say Benign

US May PCE releases at 8:30AM EDT (10:30PM AEST tonight) — the week's only hard macro catalyst. April core PCE was 3.3% YoY (0.27% MoM). Robinhood prediction markets are pricing: 94% chance above 0.2% MoM, only 39% chance above 0.3% MoM, 11% above 0.4% MoM. The modal outcome is a clean 0.2% MoM print — in-line to slightly cool relative to April.

If confirmed: US Fed July hike stays around 22–24% (no trigger pressure), hold holds at 74–76%, AUD/USD neutral. The bond market (10Y UST 4.5%) remains more hawkish than Polymarket (23.2% hike). A 0.2% MoM core print would partly resolve that divergence toward Polymarket being right. Only a hot print (≥0.3% MoM, ~3.5%+ YoY) would push hike to 27–30% and begin threatening the ↑35% trigger (11.8pp away). CME FedWatch independently shows 19.4% hike — Polymarket and CME converged at ~20-23%, prediction markets implying no movement tonight.

🌊 Story 3 — Hormuz +2pp and IRGC Bluff Called

Hormuz July 31 recovered from 46.5% (7AM) to 48.5% (+2pp) — a modest confirmation that the June 20 IRGC re-declaration has been absorbed without panic. The resolution criteria requires IMF Portwatch to publish a 7-day moving average of transit calls ≥60 ships for any date before July 31. Current transit rate: 25 ships/June 23 (5-10% of normal 100+ pre-crisis). Mine clearance is Day 7 of a 30-45 day process with 35 days remaining. The calendar is tight: full physical clearance would need to complete by late July to allow the 7-day MA to build to 60 before the deadline. Odds at 48.5% [MED quality] fairly reflect the race against the calendar.
