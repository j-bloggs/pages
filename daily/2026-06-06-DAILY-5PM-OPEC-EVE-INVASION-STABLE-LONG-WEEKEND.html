Narrative Analysis

97th Consecutive Bifurcation — Confirmed

The Iran FM's declaration of "no tangible progress, no trust" last night produced a 1pp invasion bounce (15.5% → 16.5%) — and then nothing more. Today's flat session confirms the market absorbed that statement as negotiating-lever theater, not deal collapse. The pattern now at 97 consecutive bifurcations: kinetic and diplomatic noise reprices invasion by 1-3pp in a single session, then reverts. The structural range is 15.5–17% with MOU unsigned. A signed MOU is the escape hatch.

Nuclear deal at 66.0% is the ground truth. It hasn't moved. That tells you the market believes a deal is eventually coming — the question is timing (signed this weekend vs June 10 vs later). Until the deal is signed, Hormuz stays closed, oil stays at blockade premium, and Australian petrol stays A$2.20–2.35/L.

OPEC+ June 7 (Tomorrow, Sunday)

The 41st OPEC+ Ministerial meets tomorrow in Vienna (expected online). The May 3 decision already set a 188,000 bpd increase for June (Saudi Arabia 62K + Russia 62K + others 64K). Tomorrow's expected decision: confirm 188K bpd for July as well — incremental unwinding of the 2024 production cuts, broadly in line with market expectations.

Macro impact: limited. OPEC+ production increases are practically blunted by the Hormuz closure — producers can pump more but the oil can't leave the Gulf. The real signal from tomorrow is: (1) any surprise cut would signal producers are panicking about demand, and (2) Saudi confirmation of the July increase signals they expect the MOU deal and Hormuz reopening within the next 60 days. ASX impact lands Tuesday June 10 (Monday June 8 = King's Birthday; ASX closed NSW/VIC/SA).

MOU Signature — Still the Single Most Important Binary

VP Vance's last public statement: "going back and forth on a couple of language points." Outstanding items: Hormuz reopening timeline (no tolls clause), Iran mine-clearing obligations, US port blockade suspension, sanctions waivers, 60% enriched uranium stockpile addressed in follow-on talks. None of these are novel dealbreakers — they're all known. The MOU is agreed in principle; signature is a formatting/language issue, not a substantive gap.

If Trump signs over the long weekend: expect Iran invasion to drop from 16.5% through both ↓15% and ↓10% triggers in a single session. Hormuz would spike 10–15pp. Nuclear deal would firm toward 72–75%. WTI (proxy for Brent) would fall $6–8/bbl. Australian petrol: a temporary A$0.10–0.20/L dip, but structural floor remains until physical Hormuz traffic recovers (September/October earliest).
