Cross-Market Narrative

The bifurcation pattern is bending — not breaking. For 85 consecutive data points, oil refused to confirm invasion escalation even when geopolitical noise surged. Today, oil is rising (+$3 to $104) on deal pessimism — not kinetics. This is a new flavour of the same structural theme: the conflict economy baseline is entrenching at a higher oil floor as negotiating gaps harden. Trump's "totally unacceptable" (May 11) and Iran's "never bow" creates a narrower channel for the May 23 Rome talks to navigate.


Taiwan de-escalation is the clearest signal today. This is the right macro read: if US-China are coordinating on Iran, the cost of a Taiwan escalation is higher for Beijing.
