Catalyst Deep-Dive: IAEA Non-Compliance + Iran's Response

What happened. The IAEA Board of Governors (35 members, meeting June 8–12 in Vienna) passed a US/E3 non-compliance resolution by 19 votes to 3 with 11 abstentions. The resolution demands Iran: (1) fully report its HEU stockpile (440kg of 60%-enriched uranium — weapons-grade threshold is ~90%), (2) grant inspectors access to all four enrichment facilities, and (3) restore IAEA continuity of knowledge. The resolution can also trigger UN Security Council sanctions snapback later in 2026.

Iran's response was defiance, not compliance. Tehran announced: (1) a new enrichment site will be established in a "safe zone" (hardened against airstrikes), and (2) IR-1 centrifuges at Fordow will be replaced with IR-6 models. IR-6 centrifuges produce approximately 10× the enrichment capacity of IR-1 machines. This means Iran is actively accelerating its path to weapons-grade material — not freezing it.

Market interpretation. The nuclear deal [HIGH quality] falling −4.5pp in a single session to 60.5% reflects this directly. The market is beginning to price the possibility that Iran's nuclear acceleration makes a formal deal impossible before 2027 — not yet probable (60.5% still prices deal as more likely than not), but the directional momentum is the signal.
