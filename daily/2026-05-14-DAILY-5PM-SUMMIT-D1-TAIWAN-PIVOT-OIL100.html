Summit Day 1 Intelligence: The Taiwan Pivot

Pre-summit billing vs. reality: Every analyst framed Beijing as "the Iran summit." Bessent confirmed on CNBC that Iran was the primary agenda item. Instead, Day 1 was the Taiwan summit. Xi opened by calling Taiwan "the most important issue in China-US relations" and warned of "clashes and even conflicts" if mishandled. Trump complimented Xi's leadership and said he wants a "fantastic future together." Trump himself then walked back the Iran framing: "It won't come up as much as trade."

What Xi achieved: By front-loading Taiwan, Xi got Trump to implicitly validate Taiwan as the central bilateral concern — shifting US concessions toward a Taiwan/stability framework rather than Iran/Hormuz commitments. This is a structurally negative read for Hormuz June 30: China's help on Iran now has a clearly stated price (Taiwan concessions), which Trump cannot openly pay.

What Trump achieved: Trade outcomes described by China's foreign ministry as "balanced and positive." Boeing aircraft purchases, agricultural product deals, and a "Board of Trade" framework. Musk and Jensen Huang CEO meetings described "many good things" — suggests China is opening selectively to US business interests. This is net positive for China-exposed sectors (Australian iron ore, LNG, agriculture).

The Rubio wild card: Secretary Rubio is expected to push Iran harder in working-level discussions alongside the leaders' sessions. A back-channel Xi-Iran pressure commitment remains possible — just less likely to be publicly announced. Day 2 may produce a quieter Hormuz commitment that doesn't show up in communiqués.
