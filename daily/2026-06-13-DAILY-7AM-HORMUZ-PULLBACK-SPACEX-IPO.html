Macro Narrative

WTI confirmed below $85 at $84.90/bbl — the first breach since late April — and the market is now pricing a 53% probability that WTI touches $80 before June 30. This is the single most important signal in today's briefing. Oil doesn't move 5–6% in two sessions on rumour. The geopolitical risk premium built over 110+ days of Hormuz blockade is being structurally unwound. Australian petrol is tracking toward A$2.05–2.20/L now, down from the A$2.20–2.40/L blockade premium floor. If WTI reaches $80, the Brent proxy (~$83–85) implies Australian pump prices approaching A$1.95–2.10/L — the lowest since Q4 2025.

Hormuz pulled back from its all-time high of 52% to 43.5% in the hours since the 02:53am alert, while "US x Iran diplomatic meeting" markets collapsed -13pp to -18pp overnight. This apparent contradiction resolves cleanly when you read the description of the diplomatic meeting market: it requires a deliberate face-to-face meeting between US and Iranian representatives. The Geneva June 14 format appears to have shifted toward an indirect/proximity-talks structure — both delegations present in Switzerland with Qatar/Oman as intermediaries, in separate rooms. This model (used in prior Hamas-Israel negotiations) would NOT qualify as a "diplomatic meeting" under Polymarket's criteria. Hence: diplomatic meeting markets down, permanent peace deal UP (+10pp to 52.5%, above 50% for the first time), nuclear deal stable at 74.5%. Oil is ground-truth confirmation: deals priced in, format irrelevant. The "Islamabad Declaration" can be signed without Vance and Araghchi shaking hands.

SpaceX IPO is the non-geopolitical headline of the week. SPCX closed at $161/share on June 12, $150 open, ~$2.1–2.2T market cap — one of the largest US equity offerings in history. Polymarket's $2.0–2.5T market cap bracket is at 99.9%. The risk-appetite signal is enormous: investors absorbed a $2T offering in a single session while simultaneously pricing Iran deal optimism into oil. Two independent risk-on stories — de-escalation AND historic tech IPO — converging in the same week. For the ASX Monday open, watch technology and growth names. AUD may benefit from the risk-on sentiment, partially offsetting FOMC uncertainty.

FOMC in 3 days (June 16–17 AEST): Hold locked at 99.4% — this is not the event. Warsh's first press conference as Fed Chair is. With WTI now below $85 (down from $95+ in early June), oil-driven CPI pressure is easing. Warsh may soften his stagflation language slightly — a mildly dovish signal. But US CPI remains at 3.8% YoY and the blockade premium persists structurally until Hormuz physically reopens (30+ days of mine-clearing post-signature). The AUD-relevant question: does Warsh signal any path to cuts in 2026? JPMorgan projects hold through 2026; that baseline should hold unless he surprises.
